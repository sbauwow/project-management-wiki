# Glossary

- Agentic execution: work carried out partly by autonomous or semi-autonomous software agents rather than only by humans.
- Approval gate: a deliberate checkpoint where a human must review or authorize a step before work continues.
- Context packet: the minimum useful set of instructions, files, goals, constraints, and history needed for high-quality execution.
- Critical path: the longest chain of dependent work that determines the earliest possible completion date.
- Decision log: a durable record of what was decided, why, by whom, and what assumptions were in play.
- Delegation boundary: the line between work that can be handed to an agent and work that still requires human judgment.
- Execution spine: the repeatable loop connecting goals, decomposition, execution, review, and learning.
- Failure taxonomy: a structured classification of the ways execution breaks: bad task design, bad context, tool failure, review failure, unsafe autonomy, etc.
- Human-agent operating model: the division of responsibilities between people and agents inside a work system.
- Management bandwidth: the scarce capacity to review, prioritize, decide, unblock, and maintain quality.
- Parallelism: running multiple workstreams at once to reduce cycle time, assuming coordination costs do not erase the gain.
- Portfolio: the set of initiatives competing for attention, capital, and execution bandwidth.
- Rework: effort spent correcting work that looked done but failed quality, requirements, or integration checks.
- Review load: total human effort required to inspect, validate, approve, and integrate output from others or from agents.
- Risk surface: the set of ways a project can fail or cause damage: schedule, technical, legal, security, operational, financial, reputational.
- Status theater: performative reporting that creates confidence without increasing truth.
- Telemetry: the observable traces of execution: throughput, lead time, defects, retries, review time, blocked time, and outcomes.
- Work decomposition: breaking a goal into executable pieces with clear inputs, outputs, owners, and verification rules.
