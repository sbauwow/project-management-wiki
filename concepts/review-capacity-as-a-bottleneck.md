# Review Capacity as a Bottleneck

In AI-native systems, output scales faster than judgment. That makes review capacity one of the central bottlenecks in modern project management.

## Core idea

If generation scales but verification does not, the system backs up at review.

This means teams often mistake increased agent productivity for increased project throughput. In reality, they have just moved the queue.

## Signals review is the bottleneck
- lots of work marked ready, little actually accepted
- long waits for PM, tech lead, design, security, or legal review
- rising rework caused by shallow reviews
- reviewers becoming the hidden critical path
- integration blocked on a few trusted humans

## Why it matters

Review is where organizations enforce:
- quality
- consistency
- safety
- intent
- user empathy
- business judgment

If review is overloaded, one of two things happens:
- the system slows down
- the quality bar collapses

## What good PMs do
- treat review as a schedulable resource, not invisible labor
- reduce unnecessary review surface with better task design
- standardize low-risk work so review can be lighter
- escalate only the outputs that truly require expert judgment
- monitor review queue size and turnaround time

## Failure modes
- maximizing parallel work without increasing reviewers
- asking senior people to review everything equally deeply
- no triage between low-risk and high-risk outputs
- assuming "someone will glance at it" counts as control

## Practical rule

Do not scale autonomy faster than you scale review design. If review cannot keep up, throughput is fake.
