# Project Management Anti-Patterns in Agentic Orgs

AI does not remove project management failure modes. It often amplifies them.

## Common anti-patterns

### 1. Faster chaos
Agents accelerate output, but priorities, dependencies, and ownership stay vague. Result: more artifacts, more confusion.

### 2. Fake parallelism
Too many workstreams start before key decisions settle. Result: merge debt, contradictions, wasted effort.

### 3. Drafts counted as done
Teams report generated output as finished work before review, integration, or acceptance.

### 4. Review collapse
Managerial and expert review becomes the hidden bottleneck, but planning still assumes infinite reviewer capacity.

### 5. Prompt heroics
Critical execution depends on a few magical prompts or operator habits instead of stable systems and reusable process.

### 6. No decision memory
The why behind major calls is not recorded, so agents and humans keep rediscovering or undoing past choices.

### 7. Safety theater
Teams claim there are approval gates, but in practice nobody meaningfully inspects risky outputs.

### 8. Telemetry blindness
Work is discussed through narrative updates rather than evidence: rework, blocked time, review latency, failure modes.

### 9. Over-delegation of ambiguity
Agents are handed tasks that are still too fuzzy, political, or judgment-heavy to execute reliably.

### 10. Infinite initiative sprawl
Because execution looks cheaper, organizations start too many projects and saturate decision bandwidth.

## Pattern underneath the patterns
Most failures come from one mistake: treating AI as if it only changes speed. It changes constraints, failure modes, and management design.

## Practical rule

The goal is not maximal agent activity. It is reliable outcome production under control.
