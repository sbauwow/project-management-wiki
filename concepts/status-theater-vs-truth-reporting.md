# Status Theater vs Truth Reporting

Most project reporting is designed to reduce anxiety, not increase truth. That is status theater.

Truth reporting does the opposite: it makes the real state of work legible, even when the news is uncomfortable.

## Status theater looks like
- green dashboards hiding unresolved blockers
- percent-complete numbers with no verification standard
- lots of activity updates, little discussion of actual risk
- confident language standing in for evidence
- reports optimized for stakeholder calm rather than operational control

## Truth reporting looks like
- what is actually done vs merely drafted
- what is blocked and by whom
- what assumptions are now failing
- where quality or review debt is accumulating
- what changed since the last report
- what decision is needed next

## Why this matters more with AI

AI systems can generate a lot of impressive-looking output quickly. That makes it easier to confuse:
- draft with done
- movement with progress
- polish with correctness
- throughput with validated completion

A PM who cannot distinguish these will overestimate reality every time.

## Good reporting fields
- completed and verified
- in progress and unblocked
- blocked with owner + unblock path
- review queue size
- defect/rework signals
- top 3 risks
- next required decision

## Failure modes
- reporting only task count closed
- hiding rework because it makes throughput look worse
- reporting agent output without human acceptance state
- no explicit distinction between generated, reviewed, integrated, and shipped

## Practical rule

A status update should make it easier to act. If it only makes people feel reassured, it is probably status theater.
